"VSD only motor" is a term used for motors that are exclusively designed to operate with a variable speed drive. They are not rated for continuous-duty direct-on-line operation. Often, these motors utilize different technologies than induction motors, such as synchronous reluctance or permanent magnet types. However, induction motors can also be specially designed with different winding configurations to be driven exclusively with a VSD.
VSD only motors, including both induction types and other technologies, are not included in the scope of the new regulation as they are specifically designed and specified for operation exclusively with a variable speed drive and are not rated for continuous-duty direct-on-line operation. The VSD-duty-only approach is evident on products and product documentation, clearly distinguishing them from those intended for continuous-duty direct-on-line operation.
